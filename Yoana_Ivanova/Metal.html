Metal

Forged in the late 1960s, Metal took the heavy blues of Rock and amplified it into something darker and more intense. Defined by thick distortion, thunderous drums, and virtuosic guitar solos, the genre evolved from the "doom" sounds of Black Sabbath to the high-speed aggression of Thrash. It remains a global culture focused on technical skill and raw, cathartic power.
