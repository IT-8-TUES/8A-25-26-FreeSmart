Hip-Hop

Born at a Bronx block party in 1973, Hip Hop began as a way for marginalized communities to express their reality. Built on the four pillars of DJing, MCing, Breaking, and Graffiti, it evolved from simple party chants into a complex poetic art form. By the 90s, it was a global powerhouse, and today it stands as the most influential cultural force in the world, blending storytelling with heavy bass and rhythmic precision.
